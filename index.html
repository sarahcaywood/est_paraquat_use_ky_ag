Kentucky Estimated Annual Paraquat Use, 2013 & 2017

Authored by: Sarah Caywood

Map Details

The creation of the above map was inspired by the importance of monitoring high volume herbicide and pesticide use in agriculture. One of many toxic chemicals, Paraquat, is widely used as both an herbicide and pesticide, and is classified for "resticed use" by licensed applicators by the EPA. When paraquat is ingested, human can experience toxic chemical reactions, including in the digestive orgrans, liver, kidneys, and lungs.

The state of Kentucky was chosen for this map because more than 50% of Kentucky's total land acreage is used for agriculture, and therefore, awareness of toxic chemical use is vital for public health. Due to data availbility limitations, the total annual Paraquat (EPEst-High, kg) estimations for 2013 and 2017 were selected to gain insight to how this usage changed over a period of 4 years. In 2013, the total estimated use of Paraquat in Kentucky agriculture was 122,702 (kg), while in 2017, the total estimated use of Paraquat was 67,567 (kg). This is a 55% reduction in use of this chemical in Kentucky agriculture.
